The 6th mass extinction

The Living Planet Index (LPI) measures how populations of species change over time. It highlights a stark reality: despite 30 years of efforts to curb biodiversity loss, the global LPI 2024 reveals a staggering 73% decline in monitored wildlife populations between 1970 and 2020. In just 50 years, global wildlife populations have, on average, shrunk by nearly three-quarters.
